## Selected Papers

<div class="note-card">
<h3>The Jacobian of a regular orthogonal matroid and torsor structures on spanning quasi-trees of ribbon graphs</h3>
<p>With <a href="https://sites.google.com/view/mattbakermath/home/">Matthew Baker</a> and <a href="https://dingchangxin.github.io/website/">Changxin Ding</a>. <em>Adv. Math.</em>, arXiv: <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.08796">2501.08796</a>, 2025.</p>
<p>We show that the set of spanning quasi-trees of a ribbon graph inherits a group structure from its Jacobian group without the distinguished identity element. It extends the same results for plane graphs by Chan-Church-Grochow and Baker-Wang, casting light on the role of embeddings of graphs.</p>
<p>A generalized <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Farkas%27_lemma">Farkas Lemma</a> for oriented orthogonal matroids is proved and used as a key lemma. It follows from the signed circuit axiom of oriented orthogonal matroids, as established in <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.05353">Orthogonal matroids over tracts</a>.</p>
</div>

<div class="note-card">
<h3>Baker-Bowler theory for Lagrangian Grassmannians</h3>
<p><em>Int. Math. Res. Not. IMRN</em>, April 2025. DOI: <a href="https://doi.org/10.1093/imrn/rnaf094">10.1093/imrn/rnaf094</a>, arXiv: <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.02356">2403.02356</a>.</p>
<p>This is a sequel of <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.05353">Orthogonal matroids over tracts</a>. We define antisymmetric matroids, which capture common properties of non-orientable ribbon graphs and the symplectic Grassmannian SpGr(n,2n), and develop the theory of antisymmetric matroids with coefficients.</p>
</div>

<div class="note-card">
<h3>Orthogonal matroids over tracts</h3>
<p>With <a href="https://tjin44.math.gatech.edu/">Tong Jin</a>. <em>Forum Math. Sigma</em>, August 2025. DOI: <a href="https://doi.org/10.1017/fms.2025.10085">10.1017/fms.2025.10085</a>, arXiv: <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.05353">2303.05353</a>.</p>
<p>We extend the theory of matroids with coefficients, introduced by Dress and Wenzel and developed by Baker and Bowler, to orthogonal matroids, also known as even delta-matroids.</p>
</div>
